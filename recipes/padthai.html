Pad Thai

One of the most popular dishes of Thailand. This pad thai recipe with rice noodles, eggs, tofu, and the renowned tamarind sauce creates an amazing dish that will send you straight to flavour town. It encapsulates the five flavours of Thailand, sweet, sour, salty, spicy, and umami also known as the YUM.

This recipe will forgo shrimp, and peanuts as ingredients, not only making it more accessable for those with allergies, but also easier to cook

This recipe serves ONE person.

Ingredients

  • 1 pack of dried thin rice noodles
  • 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 3 tablespoons of sugar
  • 3 tablespoons of tamarind paste
  • 3 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of Sriracha, substitute the sriracha for paprika if you are not fond of spice
  • 1 tablespoon of lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 block of tofu, cubed
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cups of bean sprouts
  • 2 green onions, greens thinly chooped, whites chooped in bigger proportions
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced or thinly chooped

Directions

  1. Place rice noodles into a bowl of room temperature water to soak; Drain the water as the noodles become al dente. 20 to 30 minutes.
  2. Whisk the sugar, rice vinegar, tamarind paste, fish sauce, lime juice, and sriracha together in a bowl until the sugar disolves.
  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan, over medium high heat. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add the tofu and cook for about 1 minute. Add 2 eggs; break the yolk, and cook till nearly done, about 2 minutes
  4. Add the soaked rice noodles, and fry for 30 seconds.
  5. Add the sauce, and stir the dish till the most of the sauce has reduced. Add the whites of the green onions, and 1 cup of the bean sprouts. Fold the noodles over the green onions, and bean sprouts, to steam them. Cook until all the sauce evaporates.
  6. Serve on a plate; garnish with green onions, bean sprouts, and lime wedges.
